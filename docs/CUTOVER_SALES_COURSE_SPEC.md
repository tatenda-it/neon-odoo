## Section A — Get in & oriented

**A1 · Log in & set your password**
Go to crm.neonhiring.com, log in with the address IT gave you, set a strong password on first login, and confirm you reach your home screen. If you can't get in, flag Robin or Tatenda before cutover.
✅ Done when: logged in, password set, apps visible.

**A2 · Your Sales Dashboard** *(verify against rep view)*
When you log in you land on your **Sales Dashboard** — your daily command centre. It shows your sales picture at a glance: pipeline value and deals by stage, leads, hot deals worth pushing, quotes that are aging (sitting too long without movement), what you've won, and your win rate — plus a tasks list and alerts. Start each morning here: it tells you what needs chasing today.
*(Your exact tiles may differ from a director's view — the dashboard adapts to your role.)*
✅ Done when: you can open your Sales Dashboard and know it's your daily starting point.

## Section B — Core daily sales tasks

**B1 · Capturing leads** *(verify)*
Every enquiry becomes a lead — if it's not in the CRM, it doesn't exist. Leads reach you two ways: **WhatsApp enquiries auto-create a lead**, and you **manually create** leads for enquiries by phone, email, or in person. Capture the client, contact, and what they need.
✅ Done when: you can create a lead manually, and know WhatsApp enquiries arrive as leads automatically.

**B2 · Working the pipeline** *(verify mechanics; stage names confirmed)*
Your deals live on the **Pipeline** as cards. Keep each deal in the stage that reflects reality — the stages are **New → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Closed Won / Lost**, plus **Payment Pending Verification**. Accurate stages keep the pipeline honest and trigger the right follow-ups. Move cards by drag-and-drop on the kanban, or via the status bar inside the deal.
✅ Done when: you can move a deal between stages both ways and know what each stage means.

**B3 · Logging client interactions** *(verify)*
Open a deal and log what happened — a call summary, a meeting note, a scheduled follow-up activity. Same-day, every time. This is how the system and your manager see a deal is being worked.
✅ Done when: you've logged a note and scheduled a follow-up activity on a deal.

**B4 · Raising a quote** *(verify; quote engine well-understood from Finance walkthrough)*
Raise a quote through the Neon quote flow. It prices from the **live catalogue / pricing rules** — not guesswork. Build the quote with line items and watch it price; it carries a currency (USD or ZWG, locked per quote) and moves through its states (Draft → Pending Approval → Approved → Sent to Client → Accepted). **Get the quote right before it goes out — when a client accepts, it sets BOTH operations and finance in motion at once** (the event starts being planned and finance picks up invoicing). A wrong quote becomes everyone's problem the moment it's accepted.
✅ Done when: you've produced a quote priced from the catalogue and understand acceptance triggers ops + finance together.

**B4a · Confirm logistics before sending a quote** *(verify exact flow; system-gate + AI reminder are a FUTURE BUILD — see roadmap note)*
Before any quote goes to a client, you must confirm the **logistics cost** and include it. Logistics is a quotable line (the **LOGISTICS** product). You don't estimate it yourself — **get the figure from Operations (currently Robin)** and add it as the logistics line. **A quote does not go out without logistics confirmed.** This matters because acceptance triggers operations and finance at once (B4) — a quote missing logistics becomes a margin problem instantly.
*(Today this is a discipline that's sometimes missed; it's being made a firm rule now, and will become a built-in system check with an AI reminder. Until then, it's on you to confirm logistics every time before sending.)*
✅ Done when: you know to get the logistics figure from Ops and add the LOGISTICS line before any quote is sent, and that no quote goes out without it.

**B5 · Finding your quotes** *(verify)*
Your quotes live under **My Quotation** — the Neon quote list, where you find, reopen, and track your own (QUO-USD / QUO-ZIG numbered, with state and total).
✅ Done when: you can list and open your own quotes.

**B6 · Discounts & the approval threshold** *(verify; threshold pending Munashe)*
A custom discount above the threshold needs **MD approval** — you can't apply it yourself. *(Threshold to be confirmed by Munashe — document says >10%.)* Know the rule before you promise a client a price.
✅ Done when: you can state the discount threshold and how approval is requested.

## Section C — Culture & expectations

*(Confirmed current by Robin, except the discount threshold in C2, pending Munashe.)*

**C1 · Communication & response standards**
- Respond to clients within **1 hour** during business hours.
- **Formal English** in all written client communication.
- **Never quote verbally** — all pricing goes through an official Neon quote.
- Send a proposal within **24 hours** of a client meeting.
- **Follow up** every proposal within **3 business days** if no response.
- **Log every client interaction same-day** in the CRM.
✅ Done when: you can recite these without looking.

**C2 · Escalation & the client promise**
- **Copy Munashe on all client email correspondence** — not just proposals, regardless of value.
- A custom discount above the threshold goes to the **MD** for approval. *(threshold pending Munashe)*
- The promise every client should feel from Neon: **Confidence, Clarity, Pride.**
✅ Done when: you know to copy Munashe on all client emails, what to escalate, and what we promise clients.
