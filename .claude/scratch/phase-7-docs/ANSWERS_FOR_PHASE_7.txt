## Purpose & scope
This document captures the design decisions that need to be locked before Phase 7 milestone build can begin. It follows the same pattern as prior phases — Phase 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 each opened with an Open Questions document, then a Schema Sketch, then milestone-by-milestone build.
Phase 7 is the Training System. The Master Plan position is after Finance (Phase 6, shipped 19 May 2026 as v17.0.7.9.0-phase6-live) and before Reports & Analytics (Phase 8). Goal: a structured way to track who at Neon is qualified to do what — covering crew competency, certifications, onboarding, and (potentially) external training, knowledge base, and event_job assignment integration.
Unlike Phase 6, Phase 7 has NOT had a pre-document framing session with Robin. Group A of this document is precisely those framing questions — they gate everything downstream. The right pattern is: Robin (and ideally Munashe) work through Group A first in a focused 30–45 min session, then the detail groups can be answered async.
### How to use this document
24 questions across 8 thematic groups. Each question states the design context, lists options where relevant, and ends with a Decision field. As decisions are made (in working sessions or async), record:
The decision itself (one of the options, or a written-out answer)

A4
Integration with event_job assignment — hard gate or soft signal?
Robin

When a sales rep or production manager assigns a crew member to an event_job role (e.g. ‘Tech for LED wall’), the system can:
(a) Hard-block the assignment if the crew member lacks the required certification/qualification. Forces compliance.
(b) Warn but allow the assignment, requiring an override reason. Pragmatic for emergencies.
(c) Just display qualification status; assignment is unaffected. Informational only.

Options:
A
Hard gate — block assignment if not qualified
B
Soft warn — allow override with documented reason
C
Informational — no gate, just visible

My instinct: B. Hard gates feel right in theory but break operational reality when someone calls in sick and a slightly-less-certified backup has to step in. Soft-warn with override reason captures both the safety signal AND the override audit trail — same posture as Phase 6’s SoD guard.

## Group B — Crew certifications & qualifications
The data model for what a crew member is qualified to do. Anchors the assignment-gating in Group G.

B1
Certification taxonomy — what gets tracked?
Robin + Ranganai

What does ‘qualified’ mean at Neon? Possible categories:
• Equipment-specific: MA3 console, DiGiCo audio desk, ChamSys, specific LED screen brand, etc.
• Role-tier: ‘Lead Tech’ (carries authority and ability), ‘Tech’, ‘Runner’, ‘Driver’.
• Safety: first-aid, work-at-heights, electrical, fire safety, vehicle licensing.
• Soft skills: client handling, language skills, leadership.
Which of these should the module track? Multi-tier taxonomy (each crew has many certifications across categories) is the flexible answer but adds schema complexity.

Options:
A
Equipment-specific only — lightest model
B
Equipment + role tier — covers the common 'who can lead' question
C
Equipment + role + safety — the comprehensive option
D
All four including soft skills

My instinct: C. Equipment + role tier + safety covers the main operational and compliance angles. Soft skills are notoriously hard to track formally and tend to live in Robin’s head better than in a database.

B
Auto-expire with warning period — standard
C
Manual marking — admin-driven

My instinct: B. Warning period (configurable per category) plus WhatsApp reminders to crew + Robin gives the best balance. Same notification infrastructure Phase 6 uses.
